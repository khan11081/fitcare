Keto Diet

The ketogenic diet is a high-fat, adequate-protein, low-carbohydrate diet that forces the body to burn fats rather than carbohydrates. The diet typically contains:

  • 70-80% of calories from fat
  • 20-25% of calories from protein
  • 5-10% of calories from carbohydrates
This diet has shown promising results for weight loss and may help improve certain health conditions. Common foods include: meat, fatty fish, eggs, butter and cream, cheese, nuts and seeds, healthy oils, avocados, and low-carb vegetables.

Mediterranean Diet

The Mediterranean diet is based on the traditional foods that people used to eat in countries bordering the Mediterranean Sea. It emphasizes:

  • Plenty of plant-based foods
  • Whole grains, legumes, and nuts
  • Replacing butter with healthy fats like olive oil
This diet is associated with a reduced risk of cardiovascular disease and overall mortality.

Vegan Diet

A vegan diet excludes all animal products, including meat, dairy, eggs, and honey. It's rich in:

  • Fruits and vegetables
  • Legumes and beans
  • Nuts and seeds
  • Whole grains
This plant-based diet can help reduce the risk of heart disease, certain cancers, and diabetes.

Paleo Diet

The paleolithic diet is based on foods similar to what might have been eaten during the Paleolithic era. A paleo diet typically includes:

  • Lean meats
  • Fish
  • Fruits and vegetables
  • Nuts and seeds
This diet excludes processed foods, grains, dairy, and legumes.
